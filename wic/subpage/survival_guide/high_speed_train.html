How To Take High-Speed Trains in China

Posted on July 16, 2024 by Wander in China
Transportation Travel Tips

Traveling by high-speed train is a fantastic way for foreign tourists to explore China – it's fast, convenient, and comfortable. This guide provides detailed information on how to navigate the process, from purchasing tickets to boarding the train.

Buying Tickets

Online Platforms

  • 12306.cn: This is China's official train ticket booking website. While the site is primarily in Chinese, you can use translation tools to help you navigate. First, you'll need to register an account using your valid passport. Once registered, you can search for train schedules, routes, and ticket availability. Enter your departure and arrival stations, travel date, and the number of passengers. The system will display the available trains and seat classes, which typically include first-class, second-class, and business-class. After selecting your preferred option, you can pay using international credit cards like Visa, Mastercard, or UnionPay.
  • Third-Party Travel Agencies: Several international travel agencies also offer booking services for Chinese train tickets. Websites like Trip.com or Trainline often have English interfaces, which can be more user-friendly for foreign tourists. These agencies usually charge a small service fee in addition to the ticket price. The booking procedure is similar: you provide your travel details, choose your train, and complete the payment.

At Train Stations

  • Ticket Offices: You can purchase high-speed train tickets at ticket offices in any major train station in China. Look for signs indicating "Ticket Office" or "售票厅" (Shòupiàotīng) in Chinese. When you go to the ticket office, present your passport to the staff and tell them your departure and arrival stations, the date of travel, and the type of seat you want. The ticket agent will then issue your ticket.
  • Self-Service Ticket Machines: Most train stations have self-service ticket machines, and many offer an English-language option. Insert your passport, follow the on-screen prompts to enter your journey details, and pay for your ticket using cash (Chinese yuan), bank cards, or mobile payment apps like Alipay or WeChat Pay.

The Boarding Process

Arriving at the Station

  • Recommended Arrival Time: It's recommended to arrive at the train station at least 30 minutes before your train's scheduled departure time for regular high-speed trains. If it's your first time at the station or if you're traveling during peak season, it's best to arrive 1 hour in advance. This will give you ample time to navigate the station, check in, and find your platform.

Security Check

  • All passengers must go through a security check. Place your luggage on the X-ray machine's conveyor belt and walk through the metal detector. Ensure you are not carrying any prohibited items, such as flammable materials, sharp objects (except for small pocket knives with blades that meet the size restrictions), or large quantities of liquids.

Ticket and ID Check

  • After the security check, you'll need to present your ticket and passport at the ticket check gates. Staff will verify your identity and ticket information. Some stations have automated ticket check gates where you can scan the QR code on your ticket or insert the ticket into the machine.

Finding the Platform

  • Once you've passed through the ticket check, look for the departure board. It will display the train number, departure time, and platform number. Follow the signs within the station to find your designated platform. Signs are typically in both Chinese and English.

Boarding the Train

  • When the train arrives at the platform, allow passengers to disembark first. Then, locate your carriage number, which is indicated by markings on the platform. Each carriage is numbered. Enter the carriage, find your assigned seat, and store your luggage in the overhead compartments or the designated luggage areas.
  • Note: Some trains with 16 carriages are configured so that there is no connecting walkway between carriages 1-8 and carriages 9-16.

During the Journey

  • Comfort and Amenities: China's high-speed trains are well-equipped for passenger comfort. Each seat has a small tray table. Power outlets are available near the seats, usually under the table or on the seat's side, so you can charge your electronic devices. Many trains also offer Wi-Fi, though the connection speed can vary. Food and beverage carts are often available, or you can purchase food and drinks in the dining car, if there is one.

By following these guidelines, you can navigate China's high-speed rail system with ease and enjoy a comfortable and efficient way to travel throughout the country.
