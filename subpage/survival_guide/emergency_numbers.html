Emergency Phone Numbers in China for Foreign Tourists

Posted on July 16, 2024 by Wander in China
Safety Travel Tips

When traveling in China, it's crucial to know the emergency phone numbers and how to reach out for help in case of unexpected situations. Here's a guide to help you stay safe.

Commonly Used Emergency Phone Numbers

110 - Police Emergency

This number is for all kinds of police-related emergencies. Whether you encounter a crime, need assistance in a dangerous situation, or have lost your belongings and suspect theft, calling 110 is the first step. The police will respond as quickly as possible to provide help and ensure your safety.

120 - Medical Emergency

In case of any medical emergencies, such as sudden illness, injury, or if someone around you is in need of immediate medical attention, dial 120. Trained medical professionals will be dispatched to the location you provide. They are equipped to handle various medical situations, from minor injuries to life-threatening conditions.

119 - Fire and Rescue

When there's a fire, a gas leak, or any situation that requires fire-fighting or rescue services, 119 is the number to call. Firefighters will rush to the scene with the necessary equipment to extinguish the fire and rescue people in danger.

How to Dial These Numbers

Using a Chinese SIM Card

If you are using a local Chinese SIM card in your mobile phone, simply dial the three-digit emergency numbers (110, 120, or 119) directly from your phone's dial pad. It's as straightforward as making any other local call.

Using a Foreign SIM Card

When using a foreign SIM card with international roaming enabled in China, the process is a bit different. First, you need to dial the international access code for China, which is +86. Then, dial the emergency number without the leading 0. For example, to call the police, you would dial +86110. Some mobile phones may automatically add the + sign when you press and hold the 0 key. Make sure your phone has sufficient credit or is on a plan that allows international calls in China. If you are not sure whether your foreign SIM card can make emergency calls in China, it's a good idea to check with your home telecom carrier before your trip.

In some cases, especially in areas with poor network coverage, you may still be able to get emergency services even if you don't have an active SIM card in your phone. Most mobile phones are designed to allow emergency calls without a SIM card as long as there is a signal from any network available.

Remember: In an emergency, stay calm and clearly communicate your location and the nature of the problem to the operator on the other end of the line. Knowing these emergency phone numbers and how to use them can make a significant difference in ensuring a safe and enjoyable trip in China.
