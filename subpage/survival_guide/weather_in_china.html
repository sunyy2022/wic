Understanding Weather in China

Posted on July 16, 2024 by Wander in China
Weather Travel Tips

China is a vast country with incredibly diverse climates, so the weather you'll experience during your travels can vary significantly depending on where you go and when. Here's what you need to know to pack appropriately and plan your trip effectively.

Seasonal Weather Overview

Spring (March - May)

  • North (e.g., Beijing): Relatively dry and windy. Temperatures rise from 5-15°C (41-59°F) in March to 15-25°C (59-77°F) in May. Mornings/evenings can be chilly.
  • South (e.g., Guangzhou, Shanghai): Often humid with occasional rain. Temperatures generally pleasant, 15-25°C (59-77°F).
  • What to Note: Dust carried by wind in the north (early spring). Sticky humidity and unpredictable rain in the south.
  • Solutions: North: Light scarf (dust), windbreaker. South: Umbrella/raincoat, lightweight/breathable clothing.

Summer (June - August)

  • North (e.g., Beijing, Tianjin): Hot and sunny, often 30-35°C (86-95°F)+. Occasional thunderstorms.
  • South (e.g., Hong Kong, Guangzhou): Hot and extremely humid, 28-33°C (82-91°F), feels hotter.
  • What to Note: Intense heat/humidity risk. Sudden, severe thunderstorms in the north.
  • Solutions: Light-colored, loose cotton/linen clothing. High-SPF sunscreen, hat, sunglasses. Small fan/misting bottle. Waterproof jacket/umbrella for northern storms.

Autumn (September - November)

  • North: Cooler and drier. Temperatures drop from 25-30°C (77-86°F) in Sept to 10-15°C (50-59°F) in Nov. Beautiful foliage.
  • South: Relatively warm (20-25°C / 68-77°F), less humid.
  • What to Note: Rapid temperature drops in the north (especially at night). Mosquitoes still present in the south.
  • Solutions: North: Layered clothing. South: Mosquito repellent, lightweight long-sleeved shirts.

Winter (December - February)

  • North (e.g., Harbin, Beijing): Cold and dry. Harbin well below -10°C (14°F) with snow. Beijing -5 to 5°C (23-41°F).
  • South (e.g., Guangzhou, Hainan): Milder. Guangzhou rarely below 10°C (50°F). Hainan warm (20-25°C / 68-77°F).
  • What to Note: Harsh cold in the north, significant indoor/outdoor temperature difference. Lack of central heating in some southern buildings can make indoors feel chilly.
  • Solutions: North: Warm winter coats, thermal underwear, hats, gloves, scarves. Dress for indoor/outdoor changes. South: Light coat/sweater. Consider a portable heater if sensitive to cold.
