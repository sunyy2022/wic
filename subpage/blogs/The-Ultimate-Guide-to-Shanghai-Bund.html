The Ultimate Guide to Shanghai's Bund: Where History Meets Modern China

The Bund (外滩, Wàitān) stands as Shanghai's most iconic landmark, where colonial-era architecture meets modern skyline along the Huangpu River. This 1.5-kilometer waterfront promenade showcases Shanghai's evolution from a modest trading port to a global financial hub.

Historical Architecture

The western bank of the Bund features 52 well-preserved buildings representing diverse architectural styles, from Gothic and Baroque to Art Deco. Notable structures include:

  • The Peace Hotel - An 80-year-old landmark offering luxury accommodation
  • Shanghai Pudong Development Bank Building - Former HSBC headquarters featuring neoclassical design
  • Customs House - Known for its iconic clock tower

Best Time to Visit

Seasonal Considerations

  • Spring (March-May): Comfortable temperatures and blooming flowers
  • Autumn (September-November): Clear skies and pleasant weather
  • Summer (June-August): Hot but vibrant atmosphere
  • Winter (December-February): Cold but festive with holiday lights

Time of Day

  • Dawn: Watch locals practicing tai chi
  • Sunset: Perfect for photography
  • Evening (19:00-23:00): Spectacular light shows illuminate both sides of the river

Essential Experiences

  • Waterfront Promenade: Take a leisurely walk along the riverside walkway
  • River Cruise: Experience the cityscape from the Huangpu River
  • Photography: Capture the contrast between historic architecture and modern skyscrapers
  • Cultural Activities: Visit the Bund History Museum
  • Dining: Sample local cuisine at various restaurants

Transportation Tips

Access the Bund via:

  • Metro: Line 2 or 10 to East Nanjing Road Station
  • Bus: Multiple routes including 20, 33, and 37
  • Ferry: Cross-river services available
